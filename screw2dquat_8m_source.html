3 % SCREW2DQUAT constructs a screw motion dual quaternion (theta in [deg]!)
4 %
5 % DQ = SCREW2DQUAT(THETA,D,AXIS,AXISPOINT) returns the dual quaternion
6 % DQ which is the dual quaternion representation of a screw motion of
7 % angle THETA and distance D around a line of axis AXIS and comprising
8 % the point AXISPOINT.
9 % - THETA represents the rotation angle [deg] around the screw axis
10 % AXIS. It is a scalar or a N-vector (the i-th element
11 % represents the angle of the i-th screw motion) where N is
12 % the number of screw motions.
13 % - D represents the translation distance along the screw axis AXIS.
14 % It is a scalar or a N-vector (the i-th element represents
15 % the distance D of the i-th screw motion) where N is the
16 % number of screw motions.
17 % - AXIS represents the screw motion axis. It is a 3-vector or a
18 % 3*N array (column i represents the rotation axis of the
19 % i-th screw motion).
20 % - AXISPOINT represents one point of the screw motion axis line.
21 % It can be any point on the line. It is a 3-vector or a
22 % 3*N array (column i represents the point coordinates of a
23 % point of the i-th screw motion axis line).
24 % - DQ is a 8*N array representing the screw motion dual quaternion
25 %
26 % DQ = SCREW2DQUAT(THETA,D,DQLINE) returns the dual quaternion
27 % DQ which is the dual quaternion representation of a screw motion of
28 % angle THETA and distance D around a line DQLINE. THETA,D and DQ are
29 % similar as above.
30 % - DQline is a line position dual quaternion. It is a 8-vector or
